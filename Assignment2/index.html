UFOs, or Unidentified Flying Objects, refer to any aerial phenomena or objects in the sky that cannot be readily identified or explained by conventional means. They are often associated with reports of sightings of unexplained or mysterious flying craft and have been the subject of speculation, investigation, and debate regarding their origin and nature.

Here is an overview of the number of UFOs reported in the last decade by each country.

In the last decade (Since 1 Jan 2012), a significant number of UFO sightings have been reported across a wide range of countries. The data reveals that the United States of America stands out with a remarkable 65,114 reported sightings, signifying a strong interest in UFO phenomena. Additionally, several other nations, including Canada, India, Russia, and multiple European countries, have also documented a substantial number of UFO sightings. This global distribution of sightings highlights the widespread curiosity and attention surrounding unidentified flying objects, reflecting their enduring presence in public consciousness.

ðŸ›¸ What shape is a UFO?

In a comprehensive analysis of reported UFO sightings, various shapes were documented, with circles and triangles being the most frequently observed, totaling 27,093 and 19,416 sightings, respectively. The data also included cigar-shaped, egg-shaped, diamond-shaped, rectangular, and disc-shaped UFOs, with varying levels of confidence in their identification. Notably, fireball and disc UFOs had high confidence levels of 0.78 and 0.81, respectively. Additionally, a category labeled other shapes accounted for 9,932 sightings.

This data underscores the diversity of UFO shapes and the complexities in ascertaining their true nature.

Military surveillance encompasses the systematic monitoring and observation activities conducted by armed forces to gather intelligence, maintain situational awareness, and enhance security through various technologies and methods, including satellites, drones, reconnaissance aircraft, and ground-based sensors.

UFOs can come under military surveillance when they are detected or reported within a military's operational area or airspace. Military organizations have radar systems, sensors, and personnel trained to monitor and investigate aerial threats or anomalies, which may include UFO sightings.
